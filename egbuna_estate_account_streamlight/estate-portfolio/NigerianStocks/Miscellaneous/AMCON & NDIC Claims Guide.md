## 📋 Understanding the Process

### What is AMCON?
**Asset Management Corporation of Nigeria** - Created in 2010 to buy bad loans and take over failed banks.

### What is NDIC?
**Nigeria Deposit Insurance Corporation** - Protects depositors and resolves failed banks since 1988.

### Realistic Expectations
⚠️ **Important Truth:** Most defunct bank shares are worthless or worth pennies on the naira.

**Why?**
- Banks failed due to insolvency (liabilities > assets)
- Depositors and creditors are paid first
- Shareholders are last in line
- Many banks had negative net worth

**Typical Recovery:**
- **Best case:** 5-15% of nominal value
- **Average case:** 0-5% or nothing
- **Timeline:** 3-10 years for resolution

**Still Worth Filing?**
- YES - If you have many shares or high nominal value
- YES - For tax documentation (capital loss)
- MAYBE - If low share count (might not be worth the effort)

## 🎯 AMCON Claims Process

### Step 1: Gather Required Documents

**Essential Documents:**
- [ ] Original share certificate(s) - MUST HAVE
- [ ] Photocopies of certificate(s) - Multiple copies
- [ ] Valid ID (International Passport, National ID, Driver's License)
- [ ] Passport photograph (2 copies)
- [ ] Proof of address (utility bill within 3 months)
- [ ] Bank account details (for payment if any)
- [ ] Purchase receipt or evidence (if available)

**Supporting Documents (if available):**
- [ ] Previous dividend warrants
- [ ] Stockbroker's confirmation
- [ ] Share register extract
- [ ] Any correspondence with the bank

### Step 2: Complete AMCON Claim Form

**Download from:** www.amcon.com.ng (look for "Claims" or "Investor" section)

**Key Sections:**
1. Personal Information
2. Bank Details (which failed bank)
3. Certificate Details (number, shares, date)
4. Contact Information
5. Bank Account (for payment)

**Critical Tips:**
- Use CAPITAL LETTERS throughout
- Fill every field - write "N/A" if not applicable
- Double-check certificate numbers
- Sign with blue ink (easier to identify as original)
- Date the form

### Step 3: Submit Your Claim

#### Option A: In-Person Submission (Recommended)

**AMCON Head Office**  
Address: AMCON Towers, 1227 Bishopsgate Street, Victoria Island, Lagos  
Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:30 AM - 4:00 PM

**What to Bring:**
- Completed claim form (2 copies)
- All original documents
- All photocopies (3 sets)
- Pen for any corrections
- Folder/envelope for organization

**At the Office:**
1. Go to reception, ask for "Shareholder Claims Department"
2. Submit your documents
3. They will review on the spot
4. Get stamped acknowledgment copy
5. Get claim reference number (WRITE IT DOWN!)
6. Ask for contact person and direct number
7. Ask for estimated timeline

## 💡 Pro Tips for Success

### 1. Be Realistic
Don't expect much. These claims are about due diligence and potential tax benefits, not wealth recovery.

### 2. Bundle Your Submission
If you have multiple banks, prepare all claims at once. Visit AMCON and NDIC on the same trip if possible.

### 3. Certified Copies
Get documents certified by:
- Notary Public (₦2,000-₦5,000 per document)
- Commissioner for Oaths
- Court Registrar

Keep originals safe!

### 4. Patience is Key
These processes take YEARS. Don't expect resolution in months.

### 5. Join Shareholder Groups
Search online for:
- "AMCON shareholder claims forum"
- "[Bank Name] shareholders group"
- Nigerian stock market forums

Learn from others' experiences.

### 6. Document Everything
- Keep copies of all submissions
- Log every phone call
- Save every email
- Note every visit

### 7. Tax Benefits
Even if you recover nothing, claims documentation can support capital loss deductions on your taxes.

### 8. Legal Assistance
If your shareholding was very valuable (millions of naira), consider hiring a lawyer who specializes in bank liquidation claims.

**Cost:** ₦100,000-₦500,000  
**Worth it if:** Claims exceed ₦1-5 million

### 9. Check Online Forums
Before submitting, search:
- "[Bank Name] liquidation update 2025"
- "AMCON [Bank Name] shareholder compensation"
- "NDIC [Bank Name] claims status"

You might find recent news or other shareholders' experiences.

### 10. Alternative: Write it Off
If your shares were few and nominal value was low, it may not be worth the time and expense. Consider:
- Document the loss for records
- Update your vault as "written off"
- Focus energy on listed and merged companies

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls

❌ **Mistake:** Sending photocopies by mail without certification  
✅ **Solution:** Always get certified true copies from notary

❌ **Mistake:** Not keeping original certificates  
✅ **Solution:** Never submit originals, only certified copies

❌ **Mistake:** No follow-up  
✅ **Solution:** Follow up every 3 months minimum

❌ **Mistake:** Wrong contact department  
✅ **Solution:** Ask specifically for "Shareholder Claims" or "Bank Liquidation"

❌ **Mistake:** Incomplete forms  
✅ **Solution:** Double-check every field before submission

❌ **Mistake:** No proof of submission  
✅ **Solution:** Always get stamped acknowledgment
