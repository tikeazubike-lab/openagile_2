## 🎯 What to Do If You Get No Response

### After 21 Days:

*Option 1: Visit in Person*
- Go to the registrar's office with all original documents
- Ask to meet with Shareholder Services
- File a formal written complaint if needed

*Option 2: Escalate to NGX*
- Contact NGX Compliance: +234 1 271 2580
- Email: compliance@ngxgroup.com
- Explain the registrar is unresponsive
- Request NGX intervention

*Option 3: Escalate to SEC*
- Securities and Exchange Commission
- Phone: +234 1 280 9002
- File a complaint about registrar non-compliance
- Website: www.sec.gov.ng

### After 60 Days:

*Legal Letter*
Consider engaging a lawyer to send a formal demand letter to:
1. The registrar
2. The successor company
3. The original company (if still exists)

Cost: Usually ₦50,000-₦150,000 for a simple shareholder dispute

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. *Be Persistent But Professional* - Follow up every 2-3 weeks

2. *Document Everything* - Keep copies of all communications

3. *Use Multiple Channels* - Phone, email, in-person visits

4. *Reference Numbers* - Always get and use reference numbers

5. *Escalation Path* - Know when to escalate (30-60 days)

6. *Batch Processing* - Contact same registrar about multiple companies at once

7. *Realistic Expectations* - Some mergers may result in share write-offs

8. *Legal Route* - Last resort, but effective if high value

9. *Join Forces* - Look for other shareholders facing same issues

10. *Stay Organized* - Use the communication tracker for each contact
